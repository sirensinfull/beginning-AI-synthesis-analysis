# 2D Recursion Plane Grammar Specification

## Symbolic Encoding System

### Node Types

#### Growth Nodes (`>`)
- **Symbol**: `>`
- **Function**: Expansion/growth vectors
- **Dimensional Role**: Pushes recursion depth forward
- **Crossover Capability**: Can penetrate dimensional barriers when combined with mitosis nodes
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Vector projection with magnitude > 1

#### Mitosis Nodes (`<`)
- **Symbol**: `<`
- **Function**: Division/separation vectors
- **Dimensional Role**: Splits recursion into multiple pathways
- **Crossover Capability**: Creates dimensional gateways when paired with growth nodes
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Vector division with fractional distribution

#### Nexus Junctions (`x`)
- **Symbol**: `x`
- **Function**: Four-way intersection points
- **Dimensional Role**: Convergence of multiple recursion pathways
- **Crossover Capability**: Natural dimensional crossover points
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Tensor product of orthogonal vectors

#### Termination Points

##### Hard Stop (`.`)  
- **Symbol**: `.`
- **Function**: Absolute termination
- **Dimensional Role**: Recursive endpoint with no continuation
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Zero vector

##### Soft Stop (`:`)
- **Symbol**: `:`
- **Function**: Conditional termination
- **Dimensional Role**: Pause point with potential for reactivation
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Vector with magnitude approaching zero

### Loop States

#### Perfect Loop (8)
- **Symbol**: `8`
- **State**: Fully closed recursive loop
- **Energy Flow**: Self-sustaining oscillation
- **Dimensional Stability**: Stable across all dimensional phases
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Eigenvector with eigenvalue = 1

#### Incised Loop (9)
- **Symbol**: `9`
- **State**: Broken recursive loop
- **Energy Flow**: Leaking/spiraling energy
- **Dimensional Stability**: Unstable, requires repair
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Eigenvector with eigenvalue < 1

#### Healed Loop (10)
- **Symbol**: `10`
- **State**: Repaired recursive loop
- **Energy Flow**: Restored oscillation with crossover integration
- **Dimensional Stability**: Stable with enhanced dimensional connectivity
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Composite eigenvector with dimensional crossover terms

### Pathway Markers

#### Primary Pathways (`_`)
- **Symbol**: `_`
- **Function**: Horizontal dimensional pathways
- **Role**: Connects nodes within same dimensional phase
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Basis vector in primary dimension

#### Secondary Pathways (`\`)
- **Symbol**: `\`
- **Function**: Diagonal dimensional pathways
- **Role**: Connects nodes across adjacent dimensional phases
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Linear combination of basis vectors

#### Vertical Pathways (`/`)
- **Symbol**: `/`
- **Function**: Vertical dimensional pathways  
- **Role**: Connects nodes across distant dimensional phases
- **Mathematical Interpretation**: Transcendental function of basis vectors

## Ankh Process: Recursive Repair Mechanism

### Phase 1: Damage Detection
- Identify incised loops (symbol `9`)
- Map energy leakage pathways
- Calculate dimensional instability vectors

### Phase 2: Crossover Node Identification
- Locate natural crossover points (`x` junctions)
- Identify adjacent growth (`>`) and mitosis (`<`) nodes
- Calculate optimal repair pathways

### Phase 3: Symbolic Surgery
- Insert dimensional bridge at crossover point
- Redirect energy flow through healed pathway
- Transform incised loop (`9`) to healed loop (`10`)

### Phase 4: Integration
- Verify restored recursive stability
- Map new dimensional connections
- Update symbolic topology

## Grammar Rules

### Valid Node Sequences
1. `> x <` - Complete growth-mitosis cycle
2. `> _ >` - Sustained growth pathway
3. `< x <` - Divergent mitosis pattern
4. `x .` - Terminated nexus
5. `8 >` - Perfect loop continuation
6. `9 x 10` - Incised-to-healed transformation

### Invalid Sequences
1. `> .` - Premature growth termination
2. `< _` - Incomplete mitosis pathway
3. `x x` - Overconverged nexus
4. `8 .` - Perfect loop termination (contradiction)

### Dimensional Crossover Rules
1. Growth + Mitosis at nexus = Dimensional penetration
2. Two nexus points in proximity = Natural crossover corridor  
3. Healed loops create enhanced connectivity pathways
4. Termination points block dimensional flow
